      <p style='text-align:left'>
        <font size='3' face='verdana, sans-serif'><b>Weight. </b>We call the gravitational force
          on an object caused by the Earth's mass the <i>weight</i> of the object (on Earth). 
        </font>
        <font style='text-align:center;background-color:transparent' face='verdana, sans-serif'>
          The weight of an object is equal to the mass of the object times the acceleration due
          to gravity on Earth: <b>g</b> (about 10 m/s<sup>2</sup>)</font>
        <font style='text-align:center;background-color:transparent' face='verdana, sans-serif'>
          :</font>
      </p>
      <div style='display:block;text-align:left'>
        <font size='3' face='verdana, sans-serif'></font>
      </div>
      <div style='display:block;text-align:center'>
        <font size='3' face='verdana, sans-serif'><b>Weight of object = mass of object * acceleration due to gravity</b></font>
      </div>
      <br>
      <div style='text-align:left'>The more mass an object has, the stronger the earth pulls on
        that object (and, because gravity is a mutually attractive force, the stronger the object
        pulls on the earth!). <span style='background-color:transparent'>(See the </span><a target='_blank'
          href='../mass-gravitationalforce/index.html'><b>
            <font color='#0000ff'>Gravitational Force</font>
          </b></a><span style='background-color:transparent'> unit for more
          information.) </span><span style='background-color:transparent'>The force that the earth
          pulls on an object on the earth is the object's weight. </span><span style='background-color:transparent'>And the
          direction of the force of weight is
          straight down (toward the center of the earth).</span></div>
